THE BEST MOVIES ACCORDING TO DAZZO

Dazzoo's top five movies of all time

Scarface

**Scarface** (1983) is a crime drama film directed by Brian De Palma and written by Oliver Stone. The film stars Al Pacino as Tony Montana, a Cuban immigrant who rises to power in the Miami drug trade during the 1980s.

Tony Montana starts with nothing, arriving in the U.S. during the Mariel Boatlift. He quickly climbs the criminal ladder, using his ruthless ambition, cunning, and violence. After earning the trust of drug kingpin Frank Lopez (Robert Loggia), Tony becomes a major player in the cocaine industry.

However, his greed and desire for more power lead to his downfall. As Tony’s empire grows, so does his paranoia and recklessness, which alienates allies and family, including his sister Elvira (Michelle Pfeiffer) and his best friend Manny (Steven Bauer).

The film is known for its graphic violence, memorable quotes ("Say hello to my little friend!"), and its exploration of the American Dream gone awry. It critiques the pursuit of wealth and power, portraying Tony's eventual destruction as a result of his unchecked ambition and moral corruption.

Though initially met with mixed reviews, **Scarface** has since become a cult classic and is regarded as one of the greatest gangster films ever made.

Good Fellas

**Goodfellas** (1990), directed by Martin Scorsese, is a crime film based on the true story of Henry Hill, played by Ray Liotta. It follows Henry’s rise through the ranks of the mafia, beginning as a young man fascinated by the criminal world. Over the years, he becomes involved with powerful mobsters like Jimmy Conway (Robert De Niro) and Tommy DeVito (Joe Pesci), but as he climbs higher, the consequences of a life in crime begin to take their toll.

The film explores themes of loyalty, betrayal, and the violent realities of organized crime. As Henry gets deeper into the mob, his life unravels, leading to his eventual cooperation with the FBI. Known for its fast-paced narrative, iconic moments, and unforgettable performances, **Goodfellas** is widely regarded as one of the greatest films of all time.

Atlantis the series

**Atlantis** (2013–2015) is a British fantasy-adventure TV series that follows the journey of a young man named Jason, who arrives in the mythical city of Atlantis and becomes entangled in various legendary events and quests. Along with his companions, Pythagoras and Hercules, Jason navigates the city’s complex politics, magical creatures, and gods, all while trying to uncover the truth about his father's mysterious disappearance.

The show blends Greek mythology with adventure and drama, offering a mix of action and humor. Throughout the series, Jason becomes a hero and plays a pivotal role in the fates of the gods and mortals.

Despite its strong fan base, **Atlantis** was cancelled after two seasons, leaving many plotlines unresolved.

300 Spartan

**300** (2006) is an epic war film directed by Zack Snyder, based on the graphic novel by Frank Miller and Lynn Varley. The movie is a stylized retelling of the historical Battle of Thermopylae, where King Leonidas (Gerard Butler) and 300 Spartan warriors stand against the massive Persian army led by King Xerxes (Rodrigo Santoro).

Spartans, known for their discipline and strength, fight to the death to protect their homeland from the Persian invasion, embodying the ideals of bravery, sacrifice, and honor. The film emphasizes the heroic and brutal nature of the battle, showcasing intense combat scenes and visually striking imagery.

While the Spartans ultimately fall in battle, their resistance inspires Greece to unite against Persia. **300** is known for its distinctive visual style, action sequences, and its portrayal of the legendary Spartan warriors.
